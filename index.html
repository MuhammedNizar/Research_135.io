LITERATURE SURVEY

The ornamental fish industry is vital to Sri Lanka's economy, but maintaining fish health and water quality poses challenges. We employ advanced techniques, including deep learning and object detection algorithms, to address these issues. By identifying fish behavior and species, we ensure precise counting and monitoring. Our machine learning models diagnose diseases early, enhancing fish health management. Real-time water quality monitoring using IoT sensors has shown significant improvements in accuracy. These methodologies aim to revolutionize ornamental fish farming through better species identification, disease control, and environmental monitoring.

The current researches focus on various approaches to evaluate aquaculture water quality and clarity utilizing advanced techniques. A deep transfer learning-based classification model was implemented in the study by Hao, Guo, Tao, and Li (2023) for analyzing water-color images for aquaculture water quality assessment. Their approach included gathering a large dataset of 5203 images and categorizing them into five classes based on the color of the water. According to the experimental results in this study, the deep learning model demonstrated its performance in classifying water quality images, achieving an accuracy rate of 99%. This research highlighted the effectiveness of deep learning approaches in automating water quality assessment tasks.

Considering all the drawbacks of the existing systems, this research proposes a solution that integrates advanced technologies such as machine learning, deep learning, and Internet of Things (IoT) devices to address key challenges in fish species and movement identification, early disease detection, and water clarity assessment in ornamental fish industry.

This section, highlights the methodologies used for each sections of this study. The mobile application associated with this project serves several key functions. Through the submission of images, videos, and other pertinent data, it enables users to identify abnormal or normal behaviors exhibited by the fish, monitor the quality of water, schedule tank cleaning dates, recognize deceased and pregnant fish, and diagnose fish diseases.

Following that, four sub components discussed:

  1. Fish movement Identification
  2. Fish species, oviparous fish and dead fish identification
  3. Early Disease detection and treatments
  4. Identifying water clarity and calculate the next tank cleaning date


Identify unusual fish behavior and investigate the factors that lead to unusual behavior. This functionality is designed to confirm whether fish behavior falls within normal parameters or exhibits abnormalities. The authors experimented with several pretrained models. Ultimately, YOLOv8 emerged as the most effective solution providing the best real-time accuracy in fish behavior detection and classification. In this effort, videos taken from different fish tank environments served as training data for the model. The training process utilized videos capturing various fish behaviors, including schooling, feeding, resting, and swimming. YOLOv8 architecture was employed for fish behavior classification and three distinct approaches were developed, through hyperparameter tuning.

Identify fish species and count.The dataset comprising 300 images, each sized 500 x 500 pixels, including three different kinds of fish: Pterophyllum (Angelfish), Poecilia reticulata (Guppy), and Carassius auratus (Goldfish). A variety of representations of each species in varied environmental situations were ensured by the use of images sourced from multiple sources. Preprocessing measures included converting the color profile to RGB format, scaling photos at a consistent resolution, and standardizing image quality to reduce variability

A component of the research setup involved the integration of various sensors, including a Salinity Sensor, Light Sensor, pH Sensor, DS18520 Digital Thermometer, and Oxygen Level Sensor. These sensors capturing relevant environmental parameters essential for disease identification and ongoing monitoring throughout the research process. Moreover, the Raspberry Pi 3 served as the preferred hardware platform, offering a adaptable and accessible computing environment

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The ornamental fisheries sector in Sri Lanka has a lot of commitment, but there are a lot of difficulties in the way of its growth and sustainability. Among these difficulties include a lack of knowledge about which fish species are best suited for various conditions and water types, a shortage of reasonably priced feed and superior brooders, and a lack of experience with managing water quality and disease prevention. Also, farmers find it challenging to maintain their businesses during low-demand times and to obtain capital for growth. Moreover, the industry's problems are made worse by restricted market access and limited government assistance. Innovative strategies are desperately needed to strengthen the industry's resilience and improve disease control procedures in light of these problems

In order to maintain the sustainability and possibility of the ornamental fisheries sector in Sri Lanka, the sector faces considerable problems in the areas of early disease diagnosis and fish behavior identification. Recent polls show that a significant portion of fish farmers (80.7%) suffer deaths of fish from a variety of causes, and nearly all fish farmers (87.7%) have trouble locating necessary chemicals and feed. Breeding activities are further limited by issues with 78.9% of the fish quality being maintained and 61.4% of the fish quality being accessed. Manufacturing difficulties are made worse by disease outbreaks (31.6%) and limited availability to medications (35.1%), while major obstacles to market access are transportation (98.2%) and market pricing fluctuation (34.5%).

With a focus on early illness diagnosis and fish behavioral recognition, the research effort aims to address the significant issues facing Sri Lanka's ornamental fisheries industry. First, the study aims to provide an understanding of the current problems by conducting a thorough look into the difficulties and obstacles faced by the business in these areas. Second, it seeks to investigate how industry procedures for disease management may be improved by incorporating cutting-edge technology like machine learning and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies. Thirdly, the study aims to create a novel model for illness detection in ornamental fish by employing modern technology in order to facilitate early detection and treatment of infectious diseases. Finally, by applying the suggested illness detection methodology to the sector, the study seeks to evaluate its ecological sustainability and financial resilience, offering insights into the industry's long-term viability and growth possibilities. The research hopes to make a substantial contribution to the growth and sustainability of Sri Lanka's ornamental fisheries sector through these goals

This study fills a significant knowledge vacuum in the ornamental fisheries sector by putting forth a novel method for identifying fish behavior and detecting illnesses early on. This study provides a comprehensive strategy to improve the resilience and viability of the sector by incorporating innovative technologies like machine 21 | P a g e learning and IoT. A major improvement in disease management techniques is represented by the suggested disease detection model, which may lower output losses and increase the sustainability of the sector as a whole. This research seeks to support the long-term development and sustainability of Sri Lanka's ornamental fisheries sector by tackling the issues raised and utilizing modern technologies.

RESEARCH GAP

According to the literature assessment, significant research gaps exist in ornamental fish aquaculture, particularly in the areas of fish behavior identification and early disease detection. First, while substantial study has been conducted on the worldwide ornamental fish market, including its size, species involved, and trade patterns, little attention has been paid to the specific difficulties confronting Sri Lanka's decorative fisheries industry. Existing research has identified issues such as stagnant fish prices, challenges in accessing export markets, and insufficient technical help in disease control and water quality management. However, there is a scarcity of comprehensive research focusing on the application of modern technologies such as machine intelligence and IoT devices to address these difficulties. Previous research has mostly focused on conventional techniques of assessing fish disease susceptibility, which frequently ignore the complex relationships between environmental factors and illness development. While several research has looked into the use of machine learning models for water quality prediction in fish farming, they have mostly focused on forecasting dissolved oxygen levels and have not addressed the broader range of water quality factors important for disease control. As a result, there is a research gap in the creation of a comprehensive disease detection model designed specifically for ornamental fish aquaculture settings, utilizing advanced technology to enable early diagnosis and treatment of infectious diseases..
